**Page Layout:** Standard; containment procedures, followed by description and experiment logs.
The true nature of the SCP is implied towards the end.

**Elevator Pitch:** The SCP is a ghost that plays a game of tag with the nearest human being, chasing them at a reasonable pace.
Upon making contact, the ghost steals the face of the human, killing them.
This human is now "It", so the ghost assumes the face of the human and continues chasing the next human target.

Containment procedures consist of a roster of D-Class personnel who take turns becoming the ghost's target,
and a remotely configurable maze that can be rearranged to keep the ghost entertained.

**Central Narrative:** The ghost is introduced as an entity that simply follows humans, removes their face, and kills them.
Without spectremetric equipment, the ghost is visible only to the nearest human being.

Experiment logs show the true nature of the ghost:

* The ghost's chasing pace is correlated with its previous victim e.g. 
  * the ghost would move at ~7m/s if it previously tagged someone physically unfit.
  * the ghost would move at ~15m/s if it previously tagged someone athletic.

* The ghost attempts to obey the physical laws of a corporeal being; it will not pass through solid surfaces while chasing a human.
  The ghost will ignore physical laws if
    * a human target is unreachable e.g. the ghost is manoeuvred into a locked room
    * the human target is 'cheating' via artificial means e.g. driving a vehicle
    * the ghost "becomes bored" - if there's no variation in the chasing environment
  
  These logs imply that the ghost has a degree of sentience.

* Finally, a D-Class personnel is given an SCP akin to the Divination Seance.
  "Haha, you're it!" is heard when the ghost makes contact with the D-class.

The containment procedure rationale becomes clearer with the experiment logs, and it is implied the ghost is playing tag.

Origin is the death of a disabled kid who often only watched other kids play.

**Hook/Attention-Grabber (Optional):** Something creepy (a face-stealing ghost) is explained by something relatively innocuous (a game of tag).

**Additional Notes (Optional):**

* Considerations to use the ghost as a limited path-finding tool to follow field agents into elusive anomalous locations such as SCP-4000 and SCP-4040.

Conprocs involve the surveillance of high-traffic public restrooms and Foundation restrooms. Neutralisation methods include "Operation Dehydration", which is only mentioned, and its details are revealed in addenda.

The description is brief, and only explains some fundamental properties of the SCP:
* The SCP is anomalous water that once ingested, compels people in restrooms to use adjacent toilets, engage in pleasant conversation and overshare their personal information.
* The SCP is neutralised if it attempts to manifest between people who cant communicate. This will hopefully make some of the conprocs click for the reader.
 

General tonal progression of the incident logs is comedic -> unsettling -> horror. Examples of logs:
* Utility provided by the SCP, such as resolving disputes and interrogation of operatives from rival organisations
* Dangers of the SCP, such as threatening the veil, and causing Foundation executives to discuss confidential information in restrooms
* "Operation Dehydration" is elaborated on. Affected civilians would be moved to a Foundation facility, and instructed to drink normal water and urinate over several days until the SCP is drained from their body. The SCP is neutralised if the people in the restroom are unable to verbally communicate, so a D-Class with a different language background would be urinating alongside the civilian.
* A town with a localised water cycle has excessive quantities of the SCP, where Operation Dehydration isn't enough to save it. The Foundation deems it to be a lost cause.

The ending suggests the SCP to be created by SCP-7939, for espionage before taking over humanity.
