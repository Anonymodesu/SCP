**Page Type:** SCP Article

**Genre:** Horror

**Page Layout:** Standard; containment procedures, followed by description and experiment logs.
The true nature of the SCP is implied towards the end.

**Elevator Pitch:** The SCP is a ghost that plays a game of tag with the nearest human being, chasing them at a reasonable pace.
Upon making contact, the ghost steals the face of the human, killing them.
This human is now "It", so the ghost assumes the face of the human and continues chasing the next human target.

Containment procedures consist of a roster of D-Class personnel who take turns becoming the ghost's target,
and a remotely configurable maze that can be rearranged to keep the ghost entertained.

**Central Narrative:** The ghost is introduced as an entity that simply follows humans, removes their face, and kills them.
Without spectremetric equipment, the ghost is visible only to the nearest human being.

Experiment logs show the true nature of the ghost:

* The ghost's chasing pace is correlated with its previous victim e.g. 
  * the ghost would move at ~7m/s if it previously tagged someone physically unfit.
  * the ghost would move at ~15m/s if it previously tagged someone athletic.

* The ghost attempts to obey the physical laws of a corporeal being; it will not pass through solid surfaces while chasing a human.
  The ghost will ignore physical laws if
    * a human target is unreachable e.g. the ghost is manoeuvred into a locked room
    * the human target is 'cheating' via artificial means e.g. driving a vehicle
    * the ghost "becomes bored" - if there's no variation in the chasing environment
  
  These logs imply that the ghost has a degree of sentience.

* Finally, a D-Class personnel is given an SCP akin to the Divination Seance.
  "Haha, you're it!" is heard when the ghost makes contact with the D-class.

The containment procedure rationale becomes clearer with the experiment logs, and it is implied the ghost is playing tag.

Origin is the death of a disabled kid who often only watched other kids play.

**Hook/Attention-Grabber (Optional):** Something creepy (a face-stealing ghost) is explained by something relatively innocuous (a game of tag).

**Additional Notes (Optional):**

* Considerations to use the ghost as a limited path-finding tool to follow field agents into elusive anomalous locations such as SCP-4000 and SCP-4040.

**Elevator Pitch:** 

A pair of researchers try to use an anomaly for the good of the general public.
This is a conflict of interest against the Foundation's principles, so they defect.

**Central Narrative:** 

Object class is shown to be reclassed from Safe to Thaumiel. The description provides some surface-level properties of the SCP, but don't reveal why it is Thaumiel.

First set of logs introduce the characters of the story.

1. Initial experiments by Jane to establish some fundamental properties of the SCP. She also enlists an engineer, Mary, to help reverse-engineer the technology.
2. An bulletin post by Jane requesting further assistance with the SCP, since she's swamped with other work.
3. Email correspondence between John and Jane, where John offers to lend a hand, and it's established that the pair were college friends.
4. Experiment logs that show John is on probation and is somewhat incompetent. Jane is shown correcting his mistakes in his experiment reports.
5. Audio logs where John asks about helping out the general public. He is shut down by Jane, who reminds him of his upcoming performance review.
6. Email from Mary, explaining failed attempts at reverse-engineering the glasses.
7. Final experiment log shows John's rejected proposal to study a dangerous sapient SCP (I'm considering SCP-096) with the glasses.

Jane takes John idea on board, and the pair is seconded to Site-322, which contains a bunch of sapient anomalies. Site-322 also happens to be John's former workplace.

1. Audio logs between John, Jane and one of John's former colleagues. The colleague reveals that during John's time in Site-322, John had unorthodox experiments with sapient anomalies, which was why he was transferred out of Site-322.
2. Audio logs between John and Jane. John brings up using the SCP to advance medical research for the visual and hearing impaired. Jane relents and starts a correspondence with their Site director to request for official permission.
3. Unpublished drafts of experiment reports found on John's hard drive. These deal with using the SCP with a variety of audiovisually impaired D-class.
4. Experiments logs cross-testing relatively safe sapient SCPs with the glasses. These experiments are headed by Jane, and they involve some fairly elaborate experiment procedures and findings. One of the SCPs is also cognitohazardous, which leads to the next section.

John and Jane return to their original workplace. Third set of experiments deal with cognitohazards, and lead into the climax of the story.

1. Email from Mary. She makes some progress analysing the radio waves emitted by the glasses.
2. Experiment logs using the SCP with man-made and anomalous cognitohazards. These show that John has improved as a researcher, and Jane no longer corrects his reports.
3. Continued email correspondence between Jane and the Site director, for permission to use their findings for the public sector. The director gradually gets impatient and dismisses Jane. This correspondence is intended to mirror Jane's first dismissal of John's ideals, and show that Jane has begun to adopt these ideals.
4. More unpublished drafts of experiment reports found on John's hard drive.
5. An email sent to an unknown address, from an unknown sender. This email contains attachments to several documents relating to the SCP, indicating someone leaked Foundation documents.
6. Audio logs of an (implied) date night between John and Jane. John reveals he is Serpent's Hand operative, and attempts to persuade Jane to his cause. Jane is furious at his deception, and it's left unclear whether John successfully persuades her.
7. Audio logs of Jane's disciplinary hearing. The earlier leak has been discovered and Jane is the prime suspect. Jane denies involvement but does not put forward John's name. She's temporarily demoted to D-class while the Foundation figures out what's up.
8. John orchestrates an experiment with SCP-096, which leads to SCP-096 breaching containment, breaking into the D-class holding cells.
9. Audio logs between Jane and John. John persuades Jane to leave with him.

With John and Jane gone, another researcher, Adam, finishes off their research.

1. Experiment logs showing that the glasses can completely suppress the cognitohazardous effects of SCP-096. Adam also transcribes a cognitohazardous document from the Serpent's Hand, found on John's hard drive.
2. Email from Adam. He explains the SCP's utility to the Foundation and requests for it be reclassed Safe -> Thaumiel.
3. Email from Mary. She posits the SCP can precisely parse brainwaves, and has potential to pick up other senses (like touch, taste) and an individual's thoughts.
4. A fictional excerpt from Google's privacy policy, stipulating that users agree to such information being collected.
